CAV-MAE-TIPS: Audio-Visual Pretraining around a Frozen Image–Text Encoder

Replacing CAV-MAE's trainable visual branch with frozen TIPSv2 — results on retrieval, zero-shot text alignment, and sound-prompted localization, plus a drift analysis and its fixes.
Edson Araujo · Goethe University Frankfurt
CAV-MAE-TIPS1
01
Setup
AudioSet-2M pretraining · 2×H100
  • Architecture: CAV-MAE two-branch ViT-B + 1 shared joint block. Visual branch = frozen TIPSv2 B/14 (448px, patch-level text alignment); audio branch + decoder init from AudioMAE (4s / 416-frame mel).
  • Losses: audio-only MAE (λ=0.5) + bidirectional CLS InfoNCE (λ=1.0) + DenseAV-style audio→patch contrastive (λ=0.2, stop-grad on visual).
  • Additions over CAV-MAE: frame-synchronized 4s audio windows; multi-positive multi-frame contrastive; anti-drift anchor on the joint block.
  • Throughput: 3.5 ms/sample vs. 43.5 of CAV-MAE-Base (~12×) — SDPA, no_grad frozen path, optimizer-state restore across chained 12h jobs.

Gate = AudioCaps caption → the model's visual CLS. It measures how much of the teacher's text alignment survives training; the untrained model gives the ceiling.
  • Freezing the encoder does not freeze the representation: the contrastive objective rotates the (trainable) joint block out of TIPS text space — gate collapses 6.2 → 0.8.
  • Audio inherits alignment only up to this drifted ceiling; the bottleneck is the target, not data.
  • Fix: a frozen copy of the joint block (7.1M) provides the TIPS-faithful visual CLS — as a drift penalty (weight λ) or as the contrastive target itself.
  • At 2M scale the anchor holds the gate on the ceiling after 3 epochs of full contrastive pressure.

05
Finding — the same drift erodes localization
ADE20K-Sound cIoU, per-epoch
  • Evaluating every epoch shows all single-positive recipes peak (~0.23 cIoU) within ~7 epochs, then erode to 0.16–0.18. Last-epoch evaluation had hidden this.
  • Multi-positive multi-frame contrastive (all frames of a clip are positives) is the effective fix: best peak 0.257, final epoch 0.225 — the decay is essentially removed.
  • Retrieval is unaffected (parity with single-frame despite 4× fewer clip negatives per batch).
  • Interpretation: per-frame positives force frame-index-specific detail into patch tokens; relaxing the positive set removes that pressure.
Finding: drift (patch axis)

Directions
  1. Finish the 2M runs: clean A epoch-25 final (today); run C to 25 epochs — if its trajectory holds, one model covers retrieval, grounding, and text alignment.
  2. Scale the contrastive batch: DDP migration for batch-512 parity with CAV-MAE-Sync (currently 48); negatives are the largest remaining retrieval lever.
  3. Breadth for the text claim: Clotho and ESC-50 zero-shot; full VGGSound test set; text-prompted (open-vocabulary) localization as the patch-level counterpart.
  4. Classification: fine-tuned AS-20K/VGGSound numbers to complement the linear probe.
  5. Analysis: dense-anchor variant (anchor the patch tokens, potentially replacing the dense loss); per-class breakdown of what audio inherits vs. what has no visual correlate.
