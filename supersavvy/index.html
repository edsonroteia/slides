Problem

SAVVY-Bench: where is the sound, in 3D?

1,522 questions over 59 egocentric clips (median 156 s). Each question is anchored to a ~1.4 s audio event — 1% of the clip. Overall score = unweighted mean of four task scores; random ≈ 28.6.

Official harness passes the whole clip (no event localization given to models). We evaluate both oracle and realistic timing; our scorer mirrors the official one exactly (random → 28.6, validated).

Why vision-only models struggle

  • When — the event is 1% of the clip
  • Behind — the wearer often cannot see the source
  • Allocentric — exo questions reference objects absent from the event frames
  • Metric — distances need geometry, not priors

Constraint

The only competent spatial model is vision-only and frozen; hardware caps it at 16 frames. All improvements must happen at test time.

Finding 3

Vision never says "behind" — classical audio corrects it

SpatialStack answers back-left/back-right once in 463 direction_ego questions (0/77 correct on back-source cases): event frames cannot see behind the wearer. An SRP-PHAT DOA expert on the 7-mic array hears it.

Two mechanisms → +2.8 overall

  • Back-override: trust DOA when it says back (fires on 40/463; 22 correct) → dir_ego .739 → .771
  • Estimator fusion: mean(SS, DOA acoustic range, 2nd SS pass) → dist_ego .580 → .663

What did not work (root-caused)

  • Confidence routing — DOA confidence does not track its correctness
  • Text hints in the prompt — the model ignores prose
  • Multi-model voting — weak models drag the strong one down
  • Range calibration — acoustic range is per-question noise (0.65 vs 1.05 m GT median)

DOA alone scores 42.3 overall — a parameter-free classical pipeline within 7 points of a 4B geometry-VLM.

Finding 4

The model wasn't failing to reason — it was reasoning blind

Exocentric questions reference scene objects ("the armchair … facing the fireplace") that are usually not in any event frame. Curation: ClipSeg retrieves the timestamps where each anchor is visible (median top score 0.86); the model gets 5 + 5 anchor frames + 6 event frames. Side effect worth noting

Relational references ("the armchair closer to the wall painting") defeat geometric grounding — ClipSeg grounds both armchairs 0.05 m apart. With curation they resolve visually: both chairs and the painting are simply in frame, and the VLM disambiguates linguistically.

Finding 5

The binding boundary: what a frozen spatial VLM can consume

Seven controlled interventions on the same model. The boundary is sharp and, we argue, defines the interface future spatial models should be trained to expose.

Self-explanatory evidence — works

  • Queried objects visible in frame → dir_exo +.045
  • Independent estimators to average → dist_ego +.083
  • Structural override where vision is blind → dir_ego +.032

Meta-referential evidence — fails

  • Text hints (DOA direction + distance in prompt): ignored
  • "These frames show what is behind you" (pose-selected): no gain
  • "Frame 0 is your viewpoint": .225 vs .339 baseline
  • … with explicit Frame-i index tokens: .238 — still fails

The experiment that hurt

Viewpoint retrieval. The wearer literally walks through the queried robot pose (median match 0.87 m / 22°; 57% of exo questions covered). Making that frame the reference should convert allocentric → egocentric, the model's strong regime (.74 vs .36).

It scores .225 — worse than not trying, in both prompt variants. The model cannot adopt a designated frame as its reference viewpoint.

Implication: frame-referential binding is a trainable interface, not an emergent one. Adding it (light fine-tune) projects this system to ≈57.

Directions

What 58+ needs

  • Instruction-bindable spatial model. Light fine-tuning for frame-referential binding ("frame 0 is your viewpoint") unlocks viewpoint retrieval — 57% coverage at ego-level accuracy projects ≈57 overall. The natural full-paper contribution; everything here becomes its motivation and ablation suite.
  • Official 32-frame configuration. Needs an 80 GB GPU (OOMs on 46/40 GB with SDPA and sharding); VSI-Bench numbers suggest real headroom.
  • Instance-level relational grounding. Detector + SAM-quality masks instead of ClipSeg blobs would revive the fully geometric exo path (its bottleneck is isolated to anchor noise).
  • Person tracking for source ranging. Naive person grounding fails in egocentric video — the wearer's own hands hijack the masks.

Claims to take away

  • Curation beats scaling at test time: +4.6 from showing a frozen model the right evidence; frame count alone adds nothing
  • Audio is a corrector, not a solver: AV models ≈ chance, yet parameter-free DOA fixes vision's one structural blind spot
  • The binding boundary is the actionable interface gap for spatial VLMs
