05Summary
AVRT Trains audio-visual reasoners by composing audio-only and vision-only reasoning traces with a text-only merger, enabling cross-modal reasoning without a native multimodal teacher.
Key contributions
  • 01Compose reasoning tracesAudio-only + vision-only teachers are merged by a text-only LLM into coherent AV reasoning traces.
  • 02Train without a native AV teacherThe first recipe for training an AV reasoner from composed single-modality reasoning supervision.
  • 03Show transferSOTA at 3B/7B, beats individual teachers, and improves audio-only and vision-only reasoning.

14Qualitative: cross-modal traces
Combines evidence

Connects visual context with audio events, not each modality alone.

Grounds sound in scene

Links bass resonance to open terrain and long-distance propagation.

Disambiguates with context

The "Jenny!" cue is meaningful only with the crowded visual scene.

Explains causal events

An off-screen firework explains why the woman startles and spills her wine.

Transfers to hard cases

Generalizes from AVQA video to OmniBench image/audio reasoning.

Takeaway: AVRT learns traces that explicitly bind auditory cues to visual evidence, and uses that binding to select the answer.

OmniBench accuracy (%) · zero-suppressed
  • Same family wins: the Qwen merger shares the student's backbone, so its token distribution aligns and the model converges to the answer format fast.
  • Cross-family hurts: Gemma's different tokenization forces a distribution the student must unlearn, dropping below the base.
Not just our quirk. Teacher–student architectural alignment is an established, recent distillation finding: VOLD (Bousselham et al., CVPR 2026) shows cold-start alignment is essential for on-policy transfer. AVRT adds an evidence point in the trace-composition setting.
Consistent with this, cross-family transfer to VideoLLaMA2 (also a Qwen-based LM) still reproduces the gain.

What AVRT shows, and where composing reasoning from single-modality teachers goes next.
Conclusion
→Composing audio-visual reasoning from single-modality teachers
  1. AVRT merges specialized audio and vision teachers with a text-only LLM into audio-visual reasoning traces, used as an SFT cold-start before GRPO, with no native multimodal teacher.
  2. State of the art at comparable size across seven benchmarks: the 3B model reaches 56.3% OmniBench, 49.2% DailyOmni, 57.3% MMAR; the 7B model improves further to 57.1%, 54.4%, 59.1%.
  3. Cross-modal training also improves single-modality audio-only and vision-only reasoning, evidence of genuine reasoning transfer.
  4. The recipe applies to any modality combination lacking reasoning data, not only audio-visual.
